What is Shooting Games?

Shooting Games is a video game genre where players participate in a battle and use guns, rifles and other types of weapons to destroy before being destroyed. In these games, players go through a range of challenges, such as close combats, shooting battles, or long-distance sniping duties. 

A shooting game comes in two forms: First-Person Shooter and Third-Person Shooter. In the former, player views the battlefield from a first-person perspective; the arms of the characters can be seen only, creating a more immersive gameplay.

On the other hand, you can see the character's full body in third-person shooters; recognizing unexpected attacks becomes easier.

Many of these games offer campaign mode besides multiplayer action in order to accustom players to the gameplay. Not only do campaign modes teach how to play, but they also get players ready for a multiplayer battle loaded with fine weapons such as pistols, rifles, RPGs, grenades and many more.

Some shooting games also offer armor and healing mechanisms to protect you from bullets or heal you after an injury. How to Play Shooting Games?

While some games have custom controls for actions, most shooter games feature more or less the same controls for essential moves. Keep in mind that the common controls we give below are a generalized list. View other controls on game pages.

You can also play some of our games on mobiles and tables. Use the virtual joystick or touchscreen to play them.

  • WASD: Move Around
  • Left Mouse Click: Shoot
  • Right Mouse Click: Aim/Zoom in
  • Space: Jump
  • Shift: Run
  • C: Crouch
  • R: Reload
  • 1-3 or Scroll: Change between weapons

Common Game Modes

Shooting games feature game modes that can be selected while creating a room. While some of them differentiate slightly, the common aims in each game mode are as follows:

  • Campaign: Single-player mode, in which players must complete missions and pass levels. The first few levels are usually tutorials.
  • Survival/Zombies/Doom's Day: Survive against increasingly difficult waves of enemies.
  • Team Deathmatch: Two teams line up. Unlimited lives. You eliminate the other team to win.
  • Free For All/ Battle Royale: You vs. everyone else. Be the last man standing.
  • Gun Game: Allows new weapons to unlock. You start with the simplest weapon and continue by upgrading.
  • Domination/ Capture the Flag (CTF): Grab the flag from the enemy's base and return it to your location. Gain points against the other team.
  • Kill Confirmed (KC): Earn points for each kill and collect dog tags. It was popularized by the Call of Duty series.
  • Arcade/Time Attack: Simpler mechanics, full access to skills and guns, instant action and no storyline.
